Bio-Calculus Interactive Application

Demonstrating the power of derivatives $f'(x)$ in Malaysian Food Security (SFM). Analyzed through the lens of Oil Palm (*Elaeis guineensis*) fresh fruit bunch yield optimization.

🌱 Scenario: Crop Growth Dynamics

In Malaysia, optimizing oil palm yield is critical for global food security and agricultural economics. After planting, an oil palm tree's Fresh Fruit Bunch (FFB) yield follows a sigmoidal growth curve as it reaches biological maturity.

By modeling this yield as a function of tree age, $f(x)$, we use its first derivative $f'(x)$ to calculate the precise rate of change in yield (tonnes/hectare/year). This reveals exactly when the plantation hits maximum productivity acceleration.

Mathematical Modeling

Yield Function $f(x)$:
$$f(x) = \frac{25}{1 + e^{-0.5(x - 7)}}$$
Where $x$ = Tree Age (years), $f(x)$ = Yield (t/ha). Base data parameters scaled from **MPOB (Malaysian Palm Oil Board) Yield Performance Statistics**.
Derivative $f'(x)$ (Rate of Growth):
$$f'(x) = \frac{12.5 \cdot e^{-0.5(x - 7)}}{(1 + e^{-0.5(x - 7)})^2}$$
Represents the instantaneous marginal yield increase per year.

📊 Realistic Data Reference: Model parameters adapted from biological trends reported in the "MPOB Oil Palm Crop Performance Data & Yield Curve Guidelines".
