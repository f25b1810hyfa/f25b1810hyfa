🌱 The Bioscience Problem

In oil palm management (Elaeis guineensis), harvesting too early results in low oil content, while harvesting too late causes fruits to drop and spoil. To maximize Malaysia's food security and export efficiency, agronomists model the growth curve of Fresh Fruit Bunches (FFB) to find the exact day where the growth rate peaks—the point of diminishing returns.

📐 The Mathematical Model

Based on localized growth dynamics from the Malaysian Palm Oil Board (MPOB) Data Portal, the FFB weight f(x) over a standard 30-day peak ripening window is modeled using a cubic polynomial:

Yield Function f(x) [kg]: f(x) = -0.002x³ + 0.09x² + 0.1x + 5
Derivative f'(x) [Growth Rate: kg/day]: f'(x) = -0.012x² + 0.18x + 0.1
